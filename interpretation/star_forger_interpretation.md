# LOGIC AND RATIONALE
Some truths and rationale, first:
- Speculative assets are first to be sold when fear hits the system.
- Speculative assets are first to be bought when fear leaves the system.
- There are varying degrees of speculative assets. Behavior of Carvana (CVNA), and Tesla (TSLA), though speculative as well in nature, will defer from the behavior of Oklo Inc. (OKLO), and Critical Minerals Corp. (CRML). This difference in behavior may be attributed to institutional holding, ETF inclusion, or simple market-cap size. The difference itself is irrelevant. What is important is your understanding that speculative assets are not all equally speculative, and thus their stock price attitude varies.

These are companies that, apart from their speculative nature, bear no semblance to one another, and yet, how is it that their stock price can exhibit similar behavior over similar timeframes? It is speculation then — or more precisely, capital's reaction to changes in speculative appetite over time.

# INTERPRETATION
While interpretation may vary from operator to operator, below is a default reading.

## INDEX
1. Rising Index
   - Speculative assets are being supported.
   - Risk appetite is growing.
   - Greed is entering the system.
   - Investors are willing to back stories / narrative over fundamentals.
   - A rising index may indicate healthy speculative confidence, early-cycle risk-on regime, or temporary euphoria.
2. Falling Index
   - Speculative assets are being abandoned first.
   - Risk appetite is shrinking.
   - Fear is entering the system.
   - Liquidity is being withdrawn from high-risk, narrative-driven assets.
   - A falling index may be an indication of stress, late-cycle risk-off rotation, or forced deleveraging..
3. Flat Index
   - Speculation is neither materially expanding nor contracting.
   - Market is in waiting, observing, digesting information, or funding selectively.
   - Neither fear nor greed is dominating.
   - Capital is discriminating.
   - A flat or neutral index may indicate a cautious market, whereby participants are adopting a wait-and-see attitude. Price movement is idiosyncratic rather than systemic.
  
## VOLUME
1. Price rising + volume rising = active buying, conviction-driven speculation.
2. Price rising + volume falling = weakening support, prone to reversals.
3. Price falling + volume rising = forced liquidation, aggressive de-risking.
4. Price falling + volume falling = abandonment, market is disengaged or bottoming out.

Star Forger specifically aggregates volume across the basket. The system uses relative volume to each stock's own history, then aggregates across the basket. This is to differentiate from one particular company's noise and broad spikes, which reflect systemic appetite or panic.

## NOTES
1. The base index is normalized, equally-weighted, and aggregated. This is done to smooth out idiosyncratic movements in favor for a more collective measurement of sentiment towards speculative assets as a whole.
2. Small oscillations in the index are expected.
3. High reading does not necessarily mean the market is overheating and a crash is imminent. A high index simply indicates speculation is being supported relative to the basket.
4. Low reading does not necessarily mean the market is bottoming out and a reversal is imminent. A low index simply indicates speculative capital is retreating first.
5. Star Forger is, first and foremost, **context**, not signal.
